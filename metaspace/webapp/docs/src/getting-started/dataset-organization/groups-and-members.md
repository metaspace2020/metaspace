# Groups & Members

## What is a Group?

In METASPACE, a group typically represents a **lab or research team**. It is
the primary unit for managing membership and sharing private data among
trusted collaborators. Any registered user can create a group.

Groups are built around trust: members of a group can see each other's
private datasets. This makes groups well suited for lab-internal
collaboration, where the PI or group admin controls who has access.

## Roles

Each group has one or more **group admins** (typically the PI or lab manager)
and one or more **members**. Admins are responsible for inviting and managing
members. All members, including admins, can see the private datasets
associated with the group.

## How to Create a Group

1. Click your username in the top-right corner and select **My groups**
2. Click **Create group**
3. Enter a group name and confirm

Once created, you become the group admin automatically.

## The Group Page

Once a group is created, its page is organized into six tabs:

| Tab | What it contains |
|---|---|
| **Description** | A free-text description of the group, editable by admins |
| **Datasets** | All datasets shared with the group |
| **Members** | Current members and their roles. Admins can invite and remove members from here |
| **Databases** | Custom metabolite databases available to the group. See [Custom Databases](/features/tools-and-integrations/custom-databases) |
| **Subscription** | The group's [METASPACE Pro](https://metaspace2020.org/plans) subscription status and management |
| **Settings** | Group name, URL slug, and other admin settings |

## How to Invite Members

1. Open your group page and go to the **Members** tab
2. Enter the email address of the person you want to invite
3. They will receive an invitation to join the group

Invitees must have a METASPACE account to accept the invitation.
